REVAMP: Reliability-Aware Dynamics-Value World Models
for VLA Policy Improvement

Anonymous Author(s)
Submitted to the 10th Conference on Robot Learning (CoRL 2026)

Abstract

Improving vision-language-action (VLA) policies beyond supervised fine-tuning (SFT) is a central bottleneck for scalable robot manipulation. Because real-robot improvement is hard to scale, recent work performs most of it inside a learned video world model. But existing world-model methods face a dilemma: they either re-weight the trajectories the demonstrations already contain—and so cannot teach new behaviors—or they explore beyond the demonstration support into regions where the world model is unreliable, a gap co-training cannot close because the policy only ever follows the model's own distribution and never visits its hallucination regions. We propose REVAMP, a unified action-and-contact-conditioned world model whose single DiT backbone produces both future-observation predictions and action-conditioned Q-values. This shared encoder is the crux of our approach: it makes the model compatible with flow-matching VLA policies via Adjoint Matching, and it supports a two-signal reliability mechanism that pairs flow-matching self-consistency (geometric faithfulness) with probe-and-inverse-dynamics verification (action faithfulness). These signals close a self-improving loop: unreliable states trigger targeted real-environment rollouts that correct the world model precisely where it was wrong, so the next round of imagined improvement can explore further without entering a hallucination region. Across three RoboCasa simulation tasks and three real-robot tasks, REVAMP improves the pretrained policy by 15 to 17 success-rate points in simulation and 25 to 28 on the real robot, outperforming both DSRL and a VLAW-style baseline on every task under an equal real-interaction budget.

Method Overview

REVAMP has three parts:

  • Unified world model. A single Wan2.2-based DiT backbone, conditioned on the action chunk and a contact signal, with two heads sharing one encoder: a dynamics head that predicts future multi-view frames and a Q-head that predicts action-and-contact-conditioned Q-values. The shared encoder lets the Q-head capture subtle contact effects such as failed grasps where the visual change is minimal.
  • Reliability-aware mechanism. Every prediction is scored by two signals: an intrinsic velocity-field self-consistency residual (geometric faithfulness) and an external visual-probe-plus-inverse-dynamics check that recovers the implied action chunk (action faithfulness).
  • Closed-loop improvement. A frequent imagination loop fine-tunes the policy with reliability-gated Adjoint Matching (QAM), while a sparse real-interaction loop triggers targeted real rollouts at flagged states and refits the world model exactly where it was unreliable.

Tasks

We evaluate REVAMP on three RoboCasa simulation tasks and three real-robot tasks, spanning articulated-object manipulation, contact-rich interaction, and long-horizon pick-and-place.

Simulation (RoboCasa)

  • TurnOnSinkFaucet — articulated-object manipulation
  • OpenCabinet — articulated-object manipulation
  • PickPlaceCounterToStove — long-horizon pick-and-place

Real robot

  • Open Jar — rotate and detach the lid
  • Stack Box — place the object and reach a stable stack
  • Fold Cloth — fold the cloth into a compact square

Results

Imagined improvement alone already lifts the pretrained π0 policy on every task with no new interaction. Two rounds of targeted online correction further raise the simulation gains, and REVAMP outperforms both DSRL and a VLAW-style baseline on every task under an equal real-interaction budget.
